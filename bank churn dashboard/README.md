
# 💳 Bank Customer Churn Dashboard (Power BI)

## 📌 Project Overview

This project focuses on understanding **why customers leave the bank (churn)** using Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and an interactive **Power BI dashboard**.

The goal is to identify key behavioral patterns that indicate churn and present them in a clear, visual format.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement

Why are customers leaving the bank?

The objective is to analyze customer data and find patterns that help identify early signs of churn.

---

## 💡 Solution Approach

* Performed EDA using Python to understand the data
* Identified key factors affecting churn
* Built a **3-page interactive Power BI dashboard** to visualize insights

---

## 📊 Dashboard Structure

### 🧾 Page 1: Overview

* Total customers, churned customers, churn rate
* Customer distribution (Existing vs Churned)
* Activity level segmentation
* Card category analysis

👉 Shows **overall churn picture**

---

### 📉 Page 2: Customer Behavior

* Transaction count and amount
* Inactivity (months inactive)
* Change in transactions over time

👉 Shows **why customers churn**

---

### 💳 Page 3: Credit Behavior

* Credit limit vs utilization (scatter plot)
* Utilization patterns
* Revolving balance analysis

👉 Shows **financial behavior patterns**

---

## 📈 Key Insights

* Around **16% of customers churn**, while most remain active
* Churn is **not driven by age or personal profile**
* Customers who churn:

  * Use the card less
  * Spend less money
  * Stay inactive for longer
  * Show declining activity over time
* Customers with **higher credit limits use a smaller portion** of their limit
* Behavioral features are **stronger indicators of churn than credit features**

---

## 🧠 Final Conclusion

Customers do not leave suddenly. They gradually reduce their usage, spending, and activity before churning. Tracking these early signals can help businesses take action and retain customers.
